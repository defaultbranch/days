AIL Madrid

I came to spent three months in the Spanish language school AIL Madrid; this is my story behind.

The Pretext

In the past I had visited Spain a few times: Seville, Andalusia, Tenerife… several weeks in total. I had also started to learn the language, first from travel guides, then from learn-at-home courses… to get more familiar with, I even had begun to watch some DVDs in that language, and had bought some books during my travels… I was a bit familiar with the language already.

In 2023, the company's project I was working for was cut short a bit; I wasn't exactly fired but I wasn't fully employed any longer either. So there was still work to do, the project still needed me short-term, but with no funds for a full salary. What began with a transition phase – to continue part-time while looking for a new job – luckily became something like a remote-work sabatical: I had not taken much vacation in the previous year, so now I went to Spain for two weeks, cramping the working hours of the first part-time month into the days before and after that trip.

And since I already was in Spain, why not also learn the language first hand? So I enrolled at a language school for the days. The experience was good, and when I returned home after these first two weeks, I negociated with my company to continue part-time from remote for three more months, allowing me to work from Spain.

This is how I ended up taking three months of Spanish classes in Spain in the morning and working remotely in the afternoons.

(One missing piece here is: After the two weeks in Spain, I and my eldest teenage daughter spent a another week in my sister's home town Gotha, and there while reflecting on the previous stay, I somehow mentioned that it would be nice to stay for longer; and then my daughter somehow continued: let's go together (…it would be unfair to leave Mom alone with all the kids; …I wanted to learn Spanish one day anyway). As it turned out: With that, we had enough pretext to go.)
